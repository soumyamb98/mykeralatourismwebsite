Pazhassi Raja Museum And Art Gallery

For all the history lovers, Calicut is the perfect place to be. The Pazhassi Raja Museum and Art Gallery is one of the best places to visit in Calicut. Named after the famous ruler Pazhassi Raja who fought the British with the help of tribes, the museum displays a variety of monuments, mural paintings, and old coins.

Location: East Hill, Kozhikode, Kerala

Thikkoti Light House

Known to have been constructed on the remains of a shipwreck, the Thikkoti lighthouse is one of the best Calicut attractions to explore. The lighthouse is located on the rocky shoreline and offers scenic views of the sea, and several species of nomadic birds. For those who’re looking forward to spend a quiet day by the sea, look no further than the Thikkoti Light House.

Location: Thikkoti Village, Kozhikode, India

Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary

The Kadalundi Bird Sanctuary is located among numerous islands near the Kadalundi River that hails from the Arabian Sea. Being a home to both local bird species as well as the migratory birds, this bird sanctuary is indeed one of the mesmerising places to visit during your Calicut sightseeing tour.

Location: Near Kadalundi Railway Gate, Kadalundi

Kozhikode

The popular travel place of Kozhikode was a leading trading center for spices during the medieval period. The district was the apple of many invaders’ eyes namely Dutch, French and later British. Many sea wars are fought to earn the rule of this priceless piece of land. As per the facts of historians, the name was derived from two words- koyil (palace) and kotta (fort). The district was once surrounded by the fort built by its ruler, the Zamorin, who encouraged trade with the Arabs by giving special concessions. It was then, Kozhikode caught the eye of many invaders. Arabs used to call it by the name Kalikat; for Chinese it was, Kalifo, and for Europeans, Calicut (Kozhikode). Right from its history to the culture, everything is distinctive about Kozhikode. It is only in Kozhikode that a woman continues to live in their ancestral house while their husbands, visit them in evening only. In fact in some areas of Kozhikode, tourists will find some colorful cross boards all depicting about facts and fiction. CSI Church, Pattalam mosque, and Commonwealth Spinning Wheel are some of the popular tourist attractions which should be witnessed on a trip to Kozhikode. Few minutes drive from Kozhikode will take tourists to a beach called Kappad where Vasco Da Gama first put his foot along with 170 other men in India. One can also visit Beypore beach that is famous for boat building yards, this place is an important destination to see during a holiday in Kozhikode. Kozhikode is ancient town with hoary past. A lot of history and beauty is associated with Kozhikode. This tourism place allows you to take a walk down the memory lane bringing you face to face to the rich heritage. Beypore – Picturesque Views

Located in the heart of Calicut, Beypore is a popular ship building port which is known to be one of the busiest fishing harbours of Kerala. Considered to be one of the best places to visit in Calicut, the Beypore beach also happens to be an important commercial center for building the ships. While the city is also famed for its unique shipping vessel called “Uru”, do not miss out on going for a ride on the famous two-kilometre long Pulimoodu bridge.

Mananchira Square

Mananchira Square tops the Calicut tourist places list. The Mananchira Square features a clearwater pool at the centre surrounded by temples, churches, and ancient homes built all around the square. Apart from that, one must visit the musical fountain, open stage, and theatre where regular shows are held to depict the historical lives of kings and palaces.

Thali Temple

If you are searching for famous temples in Kerala, Thali Temple in Calicut is the right choice for you. Located close to the Mananchira square, the Thali temple is one of the most renowned and oldest temples of Calicut. It is one of the best tourist attractions in Calicut and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. Housing a Shivalinga that measures about 2 ft., the temple is built in the typical Kerala style architecture whihch is a must visit on your next trip.

Calicut Planetarium

Established in 1997, Planetarium Calicut is one of the must-visit places on your next trip to this port city. With a capacity of about 250 seats, the Planetarium in Calicut conducts nearly 1200 shows every year. Taking the travelers through the mysteries of the universe, enjoy star gazing at its best within the walls of this epic planetarium.

Mishkal Mosque

Located at a distance of just 1.5 kilometers from the Calicut railway station, the popular tourist attraction in Calicut of Mishkal Masjid is a must visit when in the city. Featuring the intricately designed wooden pillars and doors, the beautiful flooring done with Italian tiles steals the show totally along with which, four-storeyed mosque also features a square tank resembling to the one found in temples.

Peruvannamuzhi Dam

The dam of Peruvannamuzhi can be seen enveloped within the hills and is considered to be one of the favorite picnic locations for tourists and the most popular Islands in Kerala as well as locals. To make the place more interesting, arrangements for row boating as well as speed boating have been made. The bird sanctuary, secluded islands, and a crocodile farm also make this dam one of the best tourist spots in Calicut.

Kozhippara Falls

The stunning Kozhippara Falls is located on the borders of Kakkadampoyli at the district division of Malappuram and Calicut. You will encounter numerous trekking groups here. The serene ambiance of this falls enhances the beauty of this place. Whether interested in adventurous excursions such as swimming and trekking or looking forward to witnessing the mystic beauty of Kozhikode, this place will surely surprise you with numerous experiences.

Thusharagiri Falls – Magnificent Waterfall

Thusharagiri waterfall is located in the Western Ghats of Kozhikode district. The place is crowded with numerous visitors throughout the year as the fall cascades down as three magnificent waterfalls. Thusharagiri waterfall is located in a small tribal village, sharing the name of the fall. The place is also the hub of trekkers and rock climbers. You can take an interesting trek towards Lakkidi Ghat from here.

Kakkayam – A Stunning Dam

The stunning dam site is located in Kozhikode amidst the lush greenery. The easiest way to reach here is through a bus or taxi. You can get down at Kakkayam Bus Stop and hire an autorickshaw or taxi to Kakkayam Dam. Orakkuzhi waterfall is located just next to the dam site. Don’t forget to visit this waterfall while visiting this mesmerizing dam site in Kozhikode for a perfect experience.

Mithai Theravu

Mithai Theravu, Sweet street in Kozhikode is quite popular amongst the tourists and Epicures staying in the town. It is one of the busiest places in Kozhikode and remains open until 9 PM. With an enormous variety of sweets and other delicacies, this place is truly a retreat for food-lovers.
